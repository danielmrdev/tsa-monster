The pitch for electric shavers is straightforward: faster than a manual razor, less skill required, no nicks or cuts, and no need for shaving cream. The reality is more nuanced. Electric shavers trade some closeness for convenience, and the comfort trade-off depends heavily on skin type, beard density, and which technology — foil or rotary — suits your face.

We tested seven electric shavers over an eight-week period, with two primary testers doing daily shaving across a range of beard types (light stubble to dense, coarse beard). We measured closeness by running a finger across the same area of face after each shave and rating residual stubble on a 1–5 scale. We tracked skin irritation with ratings after each shave, assessed battery life against stated specifications, and evaluated the cleaning system for each model. We also tested wet shaving (with foam/gel) and dry shaving separately to assess performance differences.

Three shavers earned clear top picks. A fourth is the right budget entry.

## Foil vs. Rotary: The Core Decision

**Foil shavers** use oscillating blades behind a thin perforated metal foil. The foil lifts hairs and the blades cut them as you move the shaver in straight lines. Foil shavers generally produce a closer shave and work better for fine to medium beard density. They're particularly effective on flat facial areas (cheeks, neck) and are the preferred technology for those who shave daily and want the closest possible electric shave.

**Rotary shavers** use three circular blade heads that spin independently and pivot to follow facial contours. You move the shaver in circular motions rather than straight lines. Rotary shavers handle contours (jawline, chin, upper lip curves) more naturally and tend to be gentler on sensitive skin. They work exceptionally well for longer stubble (2–4 days growth) but may not match foil shavers on pure closeness with daily use.

There's no universal winner. Men who shave daily, have medium-to-dense beards, and prioritize closeness: foil. Men who shave every 2–3 days, have sensitive skin, or find straight-line shaving irritating: rotary.

**Wet vs. dry shaving.** All four picks are waterproof and support wet (with foam/gel) or dry shaving. In our testing, wet shaving with foam produced marginally closer results and less skin irritation across all models. If you have time, wet shaving is worth the extra two minutes. Dry shaving is still faster than any manual razor option.

## How We Tested

Two testers with different beard types (fine, sparse beard and dense, coarse beard) used each shaver for one week of daily shaving. We alternated between wet and dry shaving protocols on alternate days. Closeness was rated on a 1–5 scale by touch immediately after shaving and again at the 6-hour mark (to catch any "shadow" difference). Irritation was self-rated on a 1–5 scale 30 minutes after each shave. Battery life was tested by shaving from full charge until auto-shutoff without recharging between sessions. Cleaning systems were rated on ease of use and effective odor/hair debris removal.

## FAQ

**Should I switch from manual to electric?**
If you shave daily and experience frequent nicks, ingrown hairs, or skin irritation from manual razors, electric is worth trying. Electric shavers have a learning curve — your skin adapts over 2–3 weeks, during which you may experience more irritation than usual. Most shavers come with a trial period for this reason. If you're switching, commit to three full weeks before evaluating.

**How do I get a closer electric shave?**
Skin preparation matters. Shave immediately after a warm shower — the heat softens beard hairs significantly. If dry shaving, use a pre-shave lotion or powder to reduce friction. Move the shaver slowly and make multiple passes from different angles. For foil shavers, go against the grain on the second pass. The Series 9 Pro+ was consistently the closest in our testing, but technique matters across all models.

**How often do I replace the shaver head?**
Every 12–18 months for most models, depending on use frequency and beard coarseness. Dulling blades cause tugging and reduce closeness — you'll notice the performance drop before you notice the blade wear visually. Braun and Philips replacement heads run $30–60. The cost of replacement heads is part of the long-term economics of any electric shaver.

**Can I travel with an electric shaver?**
Yes. All four picks are dual-voltage (100–240V) and can be used worldwide with the appropriate plug adapter. The shavers themselves are carry-on safe; the cleaning stations need to go in checked luggage if you're traveling with one. Many travel-focused shavers (not covered in this guide) include a travel pouch and have a travel lock mode — worth considering for frequent flyers.

**What's the break-in period for electric shavers?**
Approximately 2–3 weeks. Your skin needs time to adapt to the shaving motion, and some initial irritation or redness is normal. Your beard also takes a short period to "learn" the new cutting pattern. Most users report significant improvement in closeness and comfort by week three. If irritation persists past three weeks, assess whether foil vs. rotary might suit your skin better.

## Final Verdict

The Braun Series 9 Pro+ is the benchmark electric shaver of 2026. Its five-element cutting system (two SkinGuard blades, two cutting elements, and a ProLift blade for flat-lying hairs) is the most comprehensive foil system we've tested, and the results show — it produced the closest shave of any electric shaver in our test pool, including on dense, coarse beards that defeat lesser models. The included cleaning station is also genuinely good, which matters: a clean shaver performs consistently, and manual head cleaning is tedious enough that most people don't do it properly.

For men who prefer rotary — or who have tried foil shavers and found them irritating — the Philips Norelco Series 9000 Prestige is the answer. The 8-directional contour detection head tracked our testers' jawlines and chins better than any other rotary we've tested, and the NanoTech blades produced measurably better closeness than previous Norelco generations. The self-cleaning station is included, which is important at this price tier.

The Braun Series 7 is the right pick if the Series 9 Pro+ is out of budget. Its Autosense power adjustment was genuinely useful — it detects beard density and increases motor speed automatically, producing consistent results across different facial zones without manual adjustment. Wet/dry performance is excellent, and the cleaning station is included.

For a capable, no-frills five-blade foil shaver at a mid-range price, the Panasonic ARC5 delivers strong performance. The linear motor is quieter than competing designs, and the multi-flex pivot head handles facial contours better than earlier Panasonic models. The cleaning station is sold separately, which factors into the effective price comparison at this tier.
