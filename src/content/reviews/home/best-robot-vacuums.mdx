Robot vacuums have crossed a line in the last two years. The gap between marketing and reality has finally started to close — at least at the mid-to-high end of the market. We ran five robots daily for four weeks across the same floor plan: hardwood in the kitchen and living room, tile in the bathrooms, medium-pile carpet in the bedrooms, and a consistent baseline of pet hair from two medium-sized dogs. We scheduled each robot on the same run schedule and measured pickup performance, navigation reliability, and how often each one required intervention.

The biggest differentiator isn't suction power — it's navigation. Robots that build and maintain an accurate floor map navigate efficiently, avoid obstacles consistently, and don't require you to rescue them from under a coffee table three times a week. Bump-and-go robots cover the same area eventually, but inefficiently and unpredictably. For busy households, the mapping capability is worth the premium.

Mopping capability is the second emerging differentiator. Combined vacuum-mop units have gotten genuinely capable, with the Roborock S8 Pro Ultra running simultaneously — not alternating — which changes the calculus for hard-floor homes.

## App Control and Scheduling

Both the Roborock and Roomba apps are mature and well-designed. The Roborock app offers room-specific scheduling, no-go zone drawing, selective mopping zones, and cleaning history by room. The iRobot app is simpler by design — fewer settings, but the ones that matter are clearly surfaced.

**Voice assistant integration** works reliably with both Alexa and Google Home for basic commands (start cleaning, return to dock, clean a specific room). HomeKit compatibility varies by model — check current firmware, as Roborock has added Matter support to more recent units.

The Eufy RoboVac 11S has basic app scheduling but no mapping to display or room-level controls. For a budget robot, this is expected and acceptable — you schedule a time, it cleans the whole floor, it docks.

## FAQ

**What are virtual walls or no-go zones and do they work?**
Both the Roomba and Roborock use app-based no-go zones — you draw them on the floor map in the app, and the robot avoids them. This replaces the physical infrared "virtual wall" barriers that older Roombas required. They work reliably within the precision of the robot's mapping (a few inches of margin). For the Eufy without mapping, you're back to physical barriers or removing obstacles from the path.

**Can a robot vacuum handle multiple floors?**
Most mapping robots save one floor plan per dock. The Roborock S8 Pro Ultra supports multi-floor mapping — carry it to the second floor and it builds a separate map. The Roomba j7+ stores multiple maps but requires the dock to be on the floor you want to clean. Budget robots without mapping don't track which floor they're on.

**Does carpet mode boost suction for thick carpet?**
Yes. Both the Roomba j7+ and Roborock S8 Pro Ultra automatically detect carpet and boost suction when transitioning from hard floor to carpet. The Roborock also lifts the mop pad when over carpet to avoid soaking it. For very thick, high-pile carpet (over 20mm), robot vacuums generally struggle regardless of suction rating — they're designed for medium-pile and under.

**How often does the dustbin need emptying?**
Without a self-emptying base: every 1–2 runs for a pet-hair household. With a self-emptying base (Roomba j7+ and Roborock S8 Pro Ultra): the base bag lasts 60–90 days for most households. The Roborock base also automatically empties and rinses the mop reservoir, which reduces maintenance significantly.

## Final Verdict

The Roborock S8 Pro Ultra is the most autonomous floor-cleaning system available. Simultaneous vacuuming and mopping, self-emptying, self-cleaning mop, LiDAR mapping, and room-level scheduling mean this robot genuinely reduces human effort rather than just redistributing it. It's expensive, but for a household where floor cleaning is a daily task, the time savings are real.

The iRobot Roomba j7+ is the right choice for pet owners specifically. Its camera-based obstacle avoidance with AI object detection is the best we tested — it consistently avoided cords, socks, and pet toys that derailed other robots. The Pet Owner Official Promise (iRobot will replace any robot that drives over pet waste) is a real differentiator. The Eufy RoboVac 11S is the honest budget answer: it works, it's quiet, and it costs a fraction of the premium options. Run it when you leave the house, empty it when you return. For simple floor plans without obstacles, it gets the job done.
