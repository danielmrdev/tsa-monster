Air fryers went from novelty to kitchen staple fast, and for good reason. For many households, a countertop air fryer has largely replaced the oven for weeknight cooking — it preheats in under three minutes, produces genuinely crispy results without deep-frying oil volumes, and runs cooler than a full oven in summer. But not every air fryer actually delivers on those promises.

Over six months, we cooked hundreds of batches of frozen fries, fresh chicken thighs, broccoli, Brussels sprouts, and reheated leftovers in 8 different models. We tracked cooking evenness by sectioning finished food and assessing color and texture at multiple positions in the basket. We also assessed cleanup honestly — some baskets that claim to be "nonstick" required soaking after chicken. And we measured actual footprint against stated capacity to find which models earn their counter space.

Three models earned a clear recommendation. The rest either underperformed on evenness, occupied too much counter space for their capacity, or had cleanup that made us not want to use them again.

## What to Look For

**Basket vs. oven style.** Basket air fryers (the traditional design) circulate hot air around a suspended wire basket. Oven-style air fryers look like compact toaster ovens with a door and rack. Basket fryers generally produce more even crisping because airflow can surround food from below; oven-style fryers offer more flexibility for larger, flatter items. For most users cooking chicken, fries, and vegetables, a basket fryer is the better choice.

**Capacity.** Air fryers work best when the basket isn't overloaded — crowding prevents even browning. A stated 4-quart capacity practically fits about 1 lb of fries or 2 bone-in chicken thighs at a time. For 1–2 people, 3–4 quarts is ideal. For 3–5 people cooking a full meal, 5+ quarts becomes necessary.

**Wattage.** Higher wattage means faster preheat and more headroom for recovering temperature when cold food goes in. We found noticeable performance differences below 1200W — sub-1200W models tended to take significantly longer on thicker cuts. Budget models at 800W were frustratingly slow. Stay at 1200W or above.

**Presets.** Useful as starting points, not gospel. Preset programs give you a reasonable temperature and time for common foods, but you'll still need to adjust for food thickness and quantity. The shake reminder on the Cosori is genuinely useful — it beeps mid-cycle so you remember to flip or shake the basket.

## FAQ

**Is an air fryer the same as a convection oven?**
Mechanically, yes — both circulate hot air with a fan. The difference is scale and geometry. An air fryer's small chamber and close proximity to the heating element produces a more intense and concentrated airflow, which translates to faster, crispier results. A full convection oven gives you more space but the same item takes longer and often crisps less effectively than in an air fryer.

**Are air fryers actually healthier?**
Compared to deep frying, yes — a typical serving of air-fried fries uses 80–95% less oil than deep-fried equivalents. Compared to oven roasting, the calorie difference is minimal. The genuine health advantage of air frying over oven roasting is speed and convenience, which in practice means people eat home-cooked food more often instead of takeout.

**What foods work best in an air fryer?**
Anything that benefits from crisping: frozen items (fries, nuggets, fish sticks), breaded proteins (chicken cutlets, pork chops), roasted vegetables (broccoli, asparagus, Brussels sprouts), and reheated fried foods (the air fryer is dramatically better than a microwave for day-old fries). It's less suited to wet batters, large roasts, or anything that needs to steam.

**How do you actually clean an air fryer properly?**
After each use, let it cool, remove the basket, and wash with warm soapy water. Most baskets are dishwasher safe, but hand washing prolongs the nonstick coating. The inner chamber of the fryer body usually just needs a wipe with a damp cloth. Never use abrasive scrubbers on nonstick surfaces. Periodically check the heating coil (visible from above the basket slot) for grease buildup and wipe it clean with a damp cloth when the unit is fully cooled and unplugged.
